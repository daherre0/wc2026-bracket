How teams are classified & qualify
  • The 48 teams are split into 12 groups of 4 (A–L). Each team plays the other three once.
  • Match points: 3 for a win, 1 for a draw, 0 for a loss.
  • The top 2 of every group qualify automatically → 24 teams.
  • The 8 best third-placed teams across all 12 groups also advance → completing the 32-team Round of 32.
  • This table ranks those 12 third-placed teams. The 8 above the gold line are currently in qualifying positions (marked Q); the bottom 4 are eliminated as it stands.
  • Standings update live, so positions can change while matches are in progress.
Tiebreakers (applied in order)
When teams are level, FIFA separates them by:
  1. Most points
  2. Best goal difference (GF − GA)
  3. Most goals scored (GF)
  4. Then official criteria: head-to-head record, disciplinary/fair-play points, and finally drawing of lots.
This page ranks by the first three; the order within each group comes straight from ESPN's official standings.

Reading the bracket
  • The bracket lists every knockout tie. A real team appears once its group result is final; until then the slot shows a placeholder.
  • Placeholders use the feeding round: R32 / R16 / QF / SF, plus W (winner) or L (loser). Example: R32 1 W = the winner of Round-of-32 match 1.
  • Tie status: a red minute = live, FT = finished (winner in bold), a date = scheduled kick-off. Italic grey names are slots whose team isn't decided yet.
